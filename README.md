# Bachelor-Thesis-Unemployment-Insurance
# Unemployment Insurance: Effects of UI System Benefits on the Labor Market

## Overview
This repository contains my Bachelor's Thesis in Economics, titled *"Assicurazione contro la disoccupazione: Effetti dei benefici di un sistema UI nel mercato del lavoro"*. [cite_start]The research explores the fundamental trade-off between providing social insurance to unemployed workers and maintaining proper incentives for job search.

## Research Questions
* [cite_start]How do unemployment benefits influence the transition rate from unemployment to employment? 
* [cite_start]What is the impact of UI generosity on the reservation wage and search intensity? 
* [cite_start]How can the Diamond-Mortensen-Pissarides (DMP) model explain the equilibrium effects of these policies? 

## Theoretical Framework
The thesis provides an in-depth analysis of the **Search and Matching Theory (DMP Model)**, focusing on:
* [cite_start]**The Beveridge Curve:** The relationship between job vacancies and unemployment in a market with frictions.
* [cite_start]**Wage Determination:** How bargaining power and outside options (benefits) affect the final wage through the Nash Bargaining solution.
* [cite_start]**Moral Hazard:** The tension between consumption smoothing (liquidity provision) and the potential reduction in job search effort.

## Key Takeaways
1. [cite_start]**Efficiency vs. Equity:** While UI systems are essential for protecting workers against income shocks, high replacement rates can lead to longer unemployment spells due to increased reservation wages.
2. [cite_start]**Policy Design:** The study highlights the importance of benefit duration and "declining" benefit structures (lower benefits as unemployment duration increases) to balance insurance and search incentives.
3. [cite_start]**Macroeconomic Equilibrium:** Changes in UI benefits shift the labor demand and wage curves, directly impacting the steady-state unemployment rate and market tightness.
